# Why UK Landscapers Are Losing 60% of Weekend Enquiries (And How to Fix It)

It's Saturday evening. You're having dinner with your family, properly switched off from work for once. Somewhere across town, a couple is sitting in their garden talking about finally getting that patio done they've been putting off for two years.

They pull out their phone, Google "landscapers near me," find your website, and fill in your contact form. Budget's realistic. Timeline's flexible. They're ready to move forward.

What happens next?

If you're like most landscapers - including how I used to run my business - absolutely nothing. At least not until Monday morning when you're back at your desk checking emails.

By then, they've already had phone calls from three of your competitors. Two of them have given rough pricing over the phone. One's already booked a site visit for Tuesday.

You never even got a chance.

## When customers actually enquire about landscaping

I started tracking this properly in my third year of running my landscaping business. I was convinced most of my enquiries were coming in during normal working hours - you know, Monday to Friday, 9 to 5, when people had time to ring around during their lunch break.

Turned out I was completely wrong.

62% of my enquiries were coming in outside business hours. 35% at weekends. Another 27% on weekday evenings after 6pm. Peak time was Sunday afternoon between 2pm and 5pm, when people were pottering around in their gardens thinking "we really need to sort this out."

Makes perfect sense when you think about it. When are people at home looking at their outdoor space? Not Tuesday at 11am when they're at work. Saturday afternoon when they're mowing the lawn and thinking about what they'd rather be doing with that space.

So they grab their phone right then and there. Search for landscapers. Start filling in enquiry forms.

And then they wait for someone to get back to them.

## The response time problem

Here's what I didn't realise for far too long: the first business to respond wins about 50% of the time. Doesn't matter if they're the cheapest. Doesn't matter if they've got the best portfolio. First response wins.

I found a study on this a few years back - can't remember who did it now - but it tracked enquiries across different trades. Plumbers, electricians, landscapers, the lot. Same pattern every time. The business that got back to the customer first converted at nearly double the rate of everyone else.

Think about what's happening in the customer's head:

**Hour 0 (Saturday 8pm):** Really motivated. Excited about the project. Ready to get the ball rolling.

**Hour 12 (Sunday 8am):** Still keen. Checking emails to see if anyone's got back to them yet.

**Hour 24 (Monday 8pm):** Talked to two landscapers already. Both seemed decent. Getting quotes from them this week.

**Hour 48 (Tuesday 8pm):** Got one quote already. Booked site visits with two others. Pretty much decided between those three.

**Hour 72 (Wednesday 8pm):** You finally email back. Too late mate. They've mentally moved on.

The customer's excitement has a shelf life. And it's way shorter than most landscapers think.

## Why I couldn't work weekends (and why you probably can't either)

Look, I get it. You can't be on call 24/7. I tried that in my first year and nearly had a breakdown. You're running jobs during the week. You've got quotes to write up in the evenings. Weekends are for your family, for actually having a life outside work.

I wasn't going to start answering enquiry calls at 9pm on a Saturday. That's not sustainable and frankly it's not healthy.

But here's the thing - your website can work when you can't.

## The weekend I realised I needed to change something

This specific Saturday sticks in my mind. I'd had a proper day off - didn't check my work phone once. Went to my kid's football match, had lunch at the pub, came home and watched a film. Lovely day.

Monday morning I'm going through weekend emails. Seven enquiries. All came in between 10am Saturday and 6pm Sunday. Perfect timing when people are at home thinking about their gardens.

I responded to all of them first thing Monday. Professional, friendly, asked when I could come and have a look.

Two responded. Both said they'd already booked site visits with other landscapers but thanks anyway. The other five? Never heard from them again.

Seven leads. Zero conversions. All because I wasn't working on my day off.

That felt rubbish. I'd worked hard to get traffic to my website. Those enquiries weren't free - I was paying for Google Ads at the time. And they'd all slipped through my fingers because I had the audacity to have a weekend.

## What I started doing differently

I couldn't add more hours to my week. But I could change what happened when someone filled in my enquiry form.

Instead of just getting an automated "thanks for your enquiry, we'll be in touch soon" email, I set up a system that actually gave them information immediately.

They'd fill in the form - garden size, budget, what they wanted done. The system would use that information to generate a ballpark quote based on my actual pricing. Not a final number, but realistic. "Based on a 40 square metre garden with porcelain paving and artificial turf, you're looking at £9,000-12,000."

Then it would create an AI-generated design showing what their garden could look like. Photorealistic render based on the specifications they'd given.

Quote and design would hit their inbox in about 90 seconds. While they were still on my website.

Suddenly I was responding to Saturday evening enquiries on Saturday evening. Even though I was at the pub having a pint.

## The results were honestly a bit mad

First month I tried this, my weekend conversion rate went from basically zero to about 45%.

People who enquired on Saturday got a quote and a design immediately. By the time I called them Monday morning, they'd already shown the design to their partner, talked about whether the price was realistic, and decided if they wanted to move forward.

The conversation changed completely. Instead of "can you come and have a look and give us a rough idea of cost," it was "we got your quote on Saturday, that's within budget, when can you come and fine-tune it?"

I was only doing site visits with people who'd already seen realistic pricing and were still interested. Conversion rate for those site visits was about 70%. Way higher than the 40-ish percent I'd been getting before.

Same number of enquiries. Way more actual jobs.

## "But won't it feel impersonal?"

Every landscaper asks me this. I asked myself the same question before I set it up.

Here's the thing though - customers don't want to talk to you at 9pm on Saturday. They don't want to ring you and interrupt your weekend. They just want information.

They want to know if you can do what they're after and roughly what it'll cost. The instant quote gives them that. Then YOU follow up personally on Monday to have the actual conversation.

You're not replacing the personal touch. You're capturing the lead so you can have that personal conversation later.

And honestly? Customers under 50 - which is most of them now - they expect instant responses. They're used to Amazon, used to Uber, used to getting information immediately. Making them wait three days to find out if they can even afford what they want feels slow to them.

The automated quote isn't impersonal. It's convenient.

They're not working weekends. They're not staying up till midnight answering DMs. The system does the first-stage filtering for them. They just follow up with the qualified leads on Monday morning.

## The maths on weekend enquiries

Let me walk through the numbers as they were for my business. Might be different for yours, but probably not by much.

**Scenario 1: How I used to do it (no automation)**

100 weekend enquiries per year. About 2 per weekend, which sounds right.

I'd respond to them Monday morning. Maybe 30% would reply back - the other 70% had already moved on or gone with someone quicker.

Of those 30 who replied, about 40% would turn into actual jobs. So 12 jobs total.

Average job value for me was about £8,000. Total revenue from weekend enquiries: £96,000.

**Scenario 2: With instant quoting**

Same 100 weekend enquiries.

80% get an instant quote and design (the other 20% might not fill in the form properly or want something I don't do).

50% of those 80 are still engaged when I call Monday - they've seen the quote, they've discussed it with their partner, they're ready to talk about next steps.

Of those 40, about 50% convert to actual jobs. So 20 jobs.

Average job value still £8,000. Total revenue from weekend enquiries: £160,000.

That's an extra £64,000 per year from the same number of enquiries. Just by responding instantly instead of Monday morning.

And I'm not working weekends. The system is.

## What I'd tell myself if I could go back five years

Stop trying to respond to every enquiry personally. You can't work 24/7 and you shouldn't have to.

Set up a system that handles the first stage for you. Captures the lead details, gives them realistic pricing, shows them what's possible. Then YOU take over for the actual relationship building and quoting refinement.

Your job as a landscaper is to design and build amazing gardens. Not to sit at your laptop on Sunday evening responding to enquiry forms.

Automate the stuff that can be automated. Focus your time on the stuff that actually needs you.
